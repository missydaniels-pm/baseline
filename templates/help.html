What is Baseline?

Baseline is a tool for people managing chronic health conditions who want real data on what is and isn't working.

Most health trackers just log symptoms. Baseline helps you run structured experiments — introduce one change at a time, track the results, and build an evidence base for your own health decisions. Think of it as giving you the tools to be a scientist of your own health.

Getting Started

Set up what you're tracking

When you first create your account, you'll go through a short setup where you define the 1–3 symptoms you most want to track. Keep it focused — you can always add more later, but starting with your most important symptoms gives you the clearest signal.

Examples: Pain, Fatigue, Cognitive fog, Energy, Mood, Nausea, Mobility.

Establish your baseline

Before you start tracking, Baseline asks you to estimate where you are right now. How many bad days have you had in the last 4 weeks? On your worst days, how would you rate each symptom?

This gives you a starting point to measure against. It doesn't need to be perfect — a rough honest estimate is enough.

Enable AI logging

Baseline can use AI to make logging as easy as sending a text message. Instead of filling out forms, you describe your day in plain language and Baseline figures out what to record.

This is optional. If you prefer the structured forms, skip it and turn it on later in Settings. If you enable it, your messages are processed by Anthropic's API.

The Daily Check-in

How it works

If you've enabled AI logging, the Daily Check-in is your main way to log how you're doing. Instead of filling out forms, you type naturally — describe your day the way you'd tell a friend. Baseline's AI reads your message, extracts the relevant health data, and creates the right records automatically. It then confirms exactly what it logged so you can verify or correct anything.

Getting to your check-in

Tap Check-in in the sidebar to open the check-in page. If you've chatted before, you'll see your recent conversation history. If it's your first time, you'll see an empty chat ready for your first message.

What to include

You don't need to follow a specific format. Just mention whatever feels relevant:

  • Which symptoms you're experiencing and how severe they are (a rough number out of 10 helps)
  • Any medications or protocols you took — or missed
  • When things started and how long they lasted
  • How much it affected your ability to function
  • Whether you used a rescue option and if it helped
  • Any context — triggers, sleep, stress, weather, whatever seems connected

You don't need all of this every time. A quick "good day, nothing to report" is a perfectly valid check-in.

Example messages

Here are some examples of what you might say:

  • "Pretty rough today, headache around a 7, fatigue at 5, took my Sumatriptan around noon and felt better by 3pm"
  • "Good day, did all my protocols, no symptoms worth noting"
  • "Forgot my magnesium this morning, slight headache in the afternoon around a 3, managed to work through it"
  • "Woke up at a 6, been building all morning, now at an 8. Can't work. Haven't taken anything yet"
  • "Actually yesterday's headache was more like a 5, not a 7 — I think I overestimated"
  • "Took my riboflavin and CoQ10 today. No episodes. Energy was decent, maybe a 4"
  • "Bad flare since 2am, nausea at 8, completely out of commission. Used Zofran and ice pack, slightly better after 2 hours"
  • "Same as yesterday basically — low-grade headache around a 3, functioning fine"

What gets logged automatically

Based on your message, Baseline will create the appropriate records:

  • Episodes — if you describe a significant symptom event, an episode is created with onset time, duration, and functional impairment
  • Symptom scores — severity ratings are attached to the episode for each symptom you mention
  • Protocol compliance — if you mention taking or missing a medication/supplement, a compliance record is logged for that protocol
  • Rescue use — if you used a rescue option, it's recorded on the episode along with effectiveness and time to relief

After processing, the AI confirms exactly what it logged. Review the confirmation — if everything looks right, you're done.

Correcting mistakes

If the AI gets something wrong, just say so in your next message. It will update the record:

  • "Actually that headache was a 5, not a 7"
  • "I did take my magnesium today, I just forgot to mention it"
  • "That episode started yesterday evening, not today"

Check-in vs. manual logging

The check-in and the manual episode form both create the same data — episodes, symptom scores, and compliance records. Use whichever feels easier in the moment:

  • Check-in is fastest for quick daily updates and when you want to describe things in your own words
  • Manual logging (via Log Episode) is useful when you want precise control over every field, or if you haven't enabled AI logging

You can mix and match — there's no wrong approach.

Privacy and AI logging

When you use the Daily Check-in, your messages are sent to Anthropic's API for processing. Anthropic does not use your data to train models. You can read their full policy at anthropic.com/privacy.

Baseline's own privacy policy is available at Privacy Policy.

AI logging is entirely optional. You can enable or disable it at any time in Settings. If you turn it off, you can still log everything manually through the episode and protocol forms.

Logging Episodes

An episode is any significant health event worth recording — a bad day, a flare, a symptom spike. You can log episodes through the Daily Check-in or through the structured form in the Episodes section.

For each episode you can record:

  • When it started and how long it lasted
  • Severity for each of your tracked symptoms
  • How much it affected your ability to function
  • What rescue option you used (if any) and how well it worked
  • Notes, context, or anything else worth remembering

Protocols

Preventative protocols

These are the ongoing interventions you're using to manage your condition — medications, supplements, lifestyle changes, dietary protocols. Add them in the Protocols section with a start date and notes.

Each protocol has a detail page where you can log daily compliance and see a full history timeline showing every taken and missed entry alongside status changes.

Rescue options

These are the things you reach for when you're in the middle of a bad episode — medications, specific interventions, rest protocols. When you log an episode, you can record which rescue option you used and how effective it was.

Running Experiments

This is the core of Baseline. An experiment lets you test one protocol change in a structured way.

Starting an experiment

When you add a new preventative protocol, Baseline will ask if you want to track it as an experiment. You can also start one manually from the Experiments section. For each experiment you define which protocol you're testing, your hypothesis, and how long you'll give it before assessing (default is 3 weeks).

During an experiment

Keep logging as normal. The Experiments page shows your progress and how many weeks remain.

Try not to add other new preventative protocols while an experiment is active. If you change too many things at once, you won't be able to tell what's working.

Assessing the outcome

When your experiment period ends, Baseline will prompt you to assess the outcome. The assessment page shows your actual data — episode frequency before and during, symptom score changes, and a trend chart with the experiment start date marked. You'll rate how well it worked, add notes, and decide whether to continue, pause, or stop the protocol.

Over time, your completed experiments build up an evidence base — a real record of what you've tried and what the data showed.

Your Dashboard

The Dashboard is where your logging pays off. The more consistently you track, the more meaningful the data becomes. Charts and insights appear once you have at least two weeks of episodes logged.

Symptom cards

At the top of the dashboard, you'll see a card for each tracked symptom showing your average score this month. Each card also shows the percentage change from your starting baseline — so you can see at a glance if things are improving, worsening, or holding steady.

Episode frequency chart

The bar chart shows how many episodes you've had each week across the last 12 weeks. Vertical lines mark when you started each preventative protocol. This makes it easy to see whether your episode pattern changed after starting a new treatment.

Symptom trend chart

The line chart tracks how each symptom score has moved week by week over the same 12-week period. Protocol start dates are marked here too, so you can visually connect treatment changes with symptom trends.

Protocol impact markers

The vertical lines on both charts mark the start date of each preventative protocol. These matter because they let you visually answer the most important question: did things get better after I started this? Look for changes in the pattern after each marker.

Rescue effectiveness table

Below the charts, the rescue table summarises each rescue option you've used: how many times, your average effectiveness rating, and average time to relief. This helps you decide which rescue option to reach for first when you need it.

When will my charts appear?

The dashboard shows an empty state message until you have at least two weeks of data. This is intentional — the charts need enough data points to be meaningful. Keep logging consistently and the insights will build up.
